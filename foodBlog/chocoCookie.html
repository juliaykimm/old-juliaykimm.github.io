CLASSIC CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIE

back to home page

DETAILS

  • YIELDS: 14 - 20 cookies
  • TIME: 45 mintues (includes baking)

    • INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tsp warm water
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips or chopped chocoalte

    • STEPS

    1. Cream/ whip your butter and sugars together.
    2. Add eggs one at a time, mixing in between until fully combined.
    3. Add salt and vanilla.
    4. Dissolve baking soda in warm water and add
    5. Fold in your flour and then chocolate.
    6. Divide evenly on a prepared baking sheet and bake at 350° for 10-15 minutes.

    NOTES

  • Chilling your dough over night or for a few hours before baking can help your cookies bake if they are spreading out too fast. Also good for just storing them and baking when you want (freeze the dough if you do this).
  • Like any recipe, do what works for you. If there is too much baking soda and your cookies are to sour, add less!
  • BAKE BY FEEL. With some cookie recipes especially it is important to do so. I've tested a bunch where there's too much butter, so it is too runny, or too much baking soda, which makes it too sour. Just go with the flow and If you think your dough is off add whatever you feel it needs!
  • I actually messed up the dough for the cookies in this photo, I think I added too much sugar and baking soda by accident so the batter was way off to my eyes. But I just started throwing in what I thought it needed until it looked about right and they ended up being my most perfect batch!
